Theoretical Soft Matter & Biophysics Group

We are an interdisciplinary theoretical and computational research group working at the interface of soft condensed matter physics and biology. We study non-equilibrium mechanics, collective behaviors, and solid-fluid transitions in systems ranging from biological tissues to amorphous materials.

New Biophysics PhD Program at Northeastern University

A new PhD program bridging physics, biology, and chemistry to explore living systems across scales, from molecules to organisms. The program features lab rotations, training in machine learning and advanced imaging, seminars with leading researchers, and full funding packages covering tuition, stipends, and health insurance.
